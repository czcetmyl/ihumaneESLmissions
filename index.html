Who We Are

A Mission Born from the River

Along the rivers of Tonlé Sap in Cambodia, thousands of children grow up on floating villages — in communities where survival comes before school, and where dreams are a luxury few can afford.

International Humanitarian Missions (IHM) came to these children not with pity, but with purpose. For years, IHM has walked alongside the forgotten children of Tonlé Sap — bringing food, shelter, care, and most importantly, education.

Then we took it further. IHM introduced its English Lessons Program — not just teaching grammar and vocabulary, but handing these children a key to communicate with the world, to break the cycle of poverty, and to say: I am here. I have a voice. I matter.

Dignity First

Why English?

More Than a Language

For children in Tonlé Sap, learning English is a lifeline — not a subject.

🌐

Access to the World

English opens the door to global education, international jobs, and communication beyond the river.

🛡️

Protection from Exploitation

Children who can communicate and understand the world are better protected from trafficking and abuse.

💼

Economic Opportunity

English fluency creates pathways to tourism, trade, and higher-paying work — breaking the cycle of poverty.

✨

A Voice & A Dream

English gives children the confidence to dream bigger, to connect with helpers, and to imagine a life beyond the river.
